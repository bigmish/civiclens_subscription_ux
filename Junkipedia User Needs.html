Journalism & Investigative Reporting

The Political & Documentary Journalist

"I need to follow dozens of actors across platforms simultaneously — and build an organized record I can cite."

Politiken — Sofie

"I plan to use Junkipedia to monitor the cross-platform social media activity of Danish politicians, political parties, and affiliated advocacy groups. Specifically, I will use it as a primary tool during upcoming election campaigns to track political messaging, analyze digital campaign strategies, and identify potential misinformation."

When I…
cover a multi-party election campaign across four platforms
I want to…
open one saved view each morning and see everything all parties posted in the last 24 hours, ranked by engagement

Sofie covers Danish national politics for Politiken and needs to follow all nine political parties across Facebook, X, TikTok, and Instagram simultaneously during the 2026 election campaign. Rather than spending the first two hours of every morning cycling through each party's profiles manually, she wants a single view showing what everyone posted yesterday, ranked by engagement, so she can spot which party is pushing which message and catch anything that looks misleading before it spreads.

Sofie creates an Actor Set called "Danish Folketing 2026" and adds social media accounts for all nine parties. She saves a Monitoring view filtered to that Actor Set with a rolling 24-hour date window as her morning dashboard. Each day she checks the breakdown-by-channel chart to see which parties are most active and on which platforms. When the Liberal Alliance posts a series of immigration statistics that look dubious, she creates a Post List called "LA immigration claims — June 2026" and saves those posts as source material while she investigates the numbers.

Success criteria
  1. One saved view shows all nine parties' posts from the last 24 hours, ranked by engagement, every morning
  2. Breakdown chart reveals which party is most active and on which platform at a glance
  3. A Post List captures the dubious posts as source material before investigating
  4. She can filter to a single party or platform when a story requires deeper focus

Academic & Scholarly Research

The Academic Researcher

"I need systematic, gap-free data collection at scale — and a labeling pipeline that satisfies peer review."

UKP Lab · TU Darmstadt — Lukas

"We want to utilize Junkipedia to access and archive social media posts, particularly those showcasing examples of misleading content…One of our goals is to scale up their study by collecting and analyzing charts on a broader range of topics."

When I…
build a large labeled corpus for ML classifier training
I want to…
collect thousands of candidate posts and run a multi-coder labeling exercise without coders stepping on each other's work

Lukas leads a computational linguistics lab at TU Darmstadt studying how misleading data visualizations — fabricated charts, cherry-picked statistics, manipulated infographics — spread on social media. He needs a large, labeled corpus to train a classifier. Right now his team collects examples manually one by one, which doesn't scale. He needs a pipeline that collects thousands of candidate posts, labels them efficiently across multiple coders, and exports them in a format his Python ML pipeline can ingest.

Lukas creates Search Terms pairing data-visualization language — "Grafik zeigt," "data shows," "according to statistics" — with known misinformation topic keywords. The Monitoring feed collects candidate posts containing images across platforms. His three student coders work through a Labelling Exercise with a codebook for misleading visualization type: fabricated data, misleading axis, cherry-picked timeframe, out-of-context chart, genuine data. Lukas exports the labeled dataset via the API in JSON format and ingests it into his team's Python annotation pipeline.

Success criteria
  1. 2,000 candidate posts collected via Search Terms within six weeks
  2. Three coders work through the queue simultaneously without overlapping or duplicating annotations
  3. Labeled dataset exported via API with all fields (text, image URL, label, timestamp, platform) the Python pipeline expects
  4. Export filterable to a single label — e.g. pull only "fabricated data" posts for the first training run

⚠ Open question: confirm whether image files are retrievable via API, not just image URLs — for ML training this distinction matters.
